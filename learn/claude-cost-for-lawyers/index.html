How much is Claude for lawyers?

By Jordy Backes Facts checked 2026-07-19

Answer

For a lawyer, Claude is priced as a subscription. As of July 19, 2026, Anthropic lists the Pro plan at $17 a month billed annually, or $20 monthly. The Team plan, built for firms, is $20 per seat per month billed annually, or $25 monthly. A free plan exists, and heavier Max tiers start at $100.

[ The short version ]

  • On Anthropic's published prices as of July 19, 2026, Claude Pro is $17 a month billed annually and a Team standard seat is $20 per seat per month.
  • Team is the first plan the firm itself holds, for teams of 2 to 150, and it carries no model training on content by default.
  • The developer API is a separate product priced by usage. A firm working in the Claude apps does not need an API key.
  • Every plan carries usage limits, and raising them is what the Max tiers at $100 a month and up are for.
  • Prices move. Confirm the number on claude.com/pricing the day you decide, and write the date down.

The price question is usually the second one a firm asks, right after the safety question. The answer has a shape lawyers already know from other office software: a subscription per person, cheaper when billed annually, with a firm tier that adds control. This page puts the numbers on one dated table, keeps everything in subscription terms rather than API terms, and shows how to turn a seat price into a firm decision.

What Claude costs, on one dated table

Anthropic publishes one pricing page for Claude, and every number below was read from it on July 19, 2026. That dated check is the artifact this page stands on. A software price is only true with a date attached, so every figure here carries one.

Two rows deserve a second look. The Pro annual price works out to $17 a month because Anthropic bills $200 up front for the year, which matters to a firm that tracks cash monthly. And the premium Team seat at $100 sits beside the standard seat without much explanation on the card itself; what it adds is a question to settle on the pricing page the day you read it, and this page will not guess on Anthropic’s behalf.

Which plan fits a small law firm

For a solo lawyer running a first careful test, Pro does the job. It is one person’s account at one person’s price, and the test can stay on fictional files while the firm makes up its mind.

For a firm, Team is the tier built for the purpose. The seats run from 2 to 150 people, billing sits with the firm rather than with whoever signed up first, and the plan card carries the line no model training on your content by default. On Pro that protection exists as a toggle in the account holder’s settings, which is workable for a test and thin for a firm. The standard seat is cheap; control of the account is what the Team price actually buys. The settings walk-through, plan by plan, lives on Is Claude safe for lawyers?

Above Team sits Enterprise, framed by Anthropic for large businesses operating at scale and priced as a seat price plus usage at API rates. Most small firms will not start there; that plan has a page of its own: Claude Enterprise for law firms. And whether the Pro price pays for itself in a small practice is its own question, answered at Is Claude Pro worth it for lawyers?

You pay for the subscription, not for an API

Claude reaches a firm in two unrelated ways, and only one belongs in a small firm’s budget. The subscription covers the apps: claude.ai in the browser, plus the desktop and mobile apps. The API is the developer product for organizations building their own software, and Anthropic prices it by usage at API rates, the same wording its Enterprise card uses. A small firm evaluating Claude for drafting and review needs a subscription and nothing else. When a vendor quotes an AI project in tokens, that is an API conversation, and it prices a different purchase than the one on this page.

What the seat price does not cover

An honest budget names the costs that sit outside the sticker.

  • Usage limits. Every plan card on the pricing page notes that usage limits apply, with a link to Anthropic’s own guidance. A firm that keeps meeting the ceiling has found the case for Max: from $100 a month, at 5x or 20x more usage than Pro. Meet the limit first, then pay to raise it.
  • The reviewing hour. Claude drafts; a licensed attorney reviews and approves everything before it leaves the firm. Review-ready, never send-ready. That reviewing hour stays a real cost the subscription does not remove, and it belongs in the same budget line as supervising fast junior work.
  • Add-ons that may ride along. Anthropic ships a legal plugin that installs in Claude Cowork, and its page names no separate price as of the check date above. Re-read that page beside the pricing page on the day you subscribe, since packaging moves.

Put a number on it for your firm

Four steps turn the table above into a monthly figure the partners can argue about.

  1. 01

    Count the seats

    List the people who would use Claude in a normal week: the lawyers, the paralegal who drafts, whoever runs intake. Team pricing is per seat, and the plan covers teams of 2 to 150, so this count is most of the math.

    Result: A seat count you can defend at the partner meeting.

  2. 02

    Pick the plan class

    One lawyer testing on fictional files can start on Pro at $17 a month billed annually. A firm putting client work through it wants Team, because the firm rather than one person holds the plan, and the pricing card carries no model training on your content by default.

    Result: A plan class matched to who controls the account.

  3. 03

    Multiply, then set it against one billed hour

    Seats times seat price gives the monthly figure. Hold that number against what one billed hour brings into the firm; the comparison, and not the sticker, is the decision.

    Result: The firm's monthly cost, stated in firm terms.

  4. 04

    Confirm the price on the day you subscribe

    Open claude.com/pricing, read the plan card you picked, and note the price with the date in the firm file. The prices on this page were read on July 19, 2026, and this page only claims them for that date.

    Result: A dated record of what the firm agreed to pay.

Before the firm pays

The checks below can run in any order, and each one points back to the place on this page that settles it.

[ Checklist ]

Answer these before the firm pays

  • Who holds the plan. A firm plan puts billing and the seats with the firm; a personal plan leaves them with one person. The plan section above weighs this.

  • Seat count and seat type. How many standard seats the firm needs, and whether anyone has a case for the premium seat. Step 1 above builds the count.

  • Training posture, in writing. Team and Enterprise carry no model training on content by default; on a personal plan the toggle sits with the account holder. The plan section above covers which class does what.

  • Annual or monthly billing. The annual seat price is lower each month; both prices sit on the dated table. The table above carries both.

  • The date of the price check. Prices move, and a dated note in the firm file settles later questions. Step 4 above makes the record.

Write the answers down. A pricing decision that lives in one partner’s memory gets re-argued every quarter.

08 / FAQ

The questions, answered

How much does Claude cost for a small law firm per month?

Take the Team standard seat at $20 per seat per month billed annually, the price Anthropic listed on July 19, 2026, and multiply by the people who will use it in a normal week. A firm of three pays for three seats. The premium seat, at $100, exists for heavier use, and most small firms can start without it.

What is the difference between Claude Pro and Claude Team for a law firm?

Pro is one person's subscription at $17 a month billed annually. Team starts at $20 per seat per month, covers teams of 2 to 150, and the plan sits with the firm rather than with one person. Team also carries the line no model training on your content by default; on Pro that choice is a setting the account holder controls.

Do lawyers need the Claude API?

No, work in the Claude apps runs on the subscription. The API is a developer product for firms building their own software, and Anthropic prices it by usage at API rates rather than by seat. If nobody at the firm is writing code against Claude, an API key adds cost and setup with no benefit for daily drafting.

Is the free Claude plan enough for a lawyer?

It is enough to learn on fictional files. The free plan runs under the same consumer terms as Pro, usage limits apply on it as on every plan, and the training choice sits in the account holder's settings. Client work belongs on a plan the firm controls, under terms someone at the firm has read.

Does the Claude legal plugin cost extra?

Anthropic's page for the legal plugin names no separate price. The plugin is made by Anthropic and installs in Claude Cowork. Read the plugin page beside the pricing page on the day you decide, because packaging like this tends to move faster than the plan prices do.
